Cost Driver

Cost drivers are a high-level cost calculators primarily used for reporting. These drivers are associated with price list items and services. When associated with a service, they define the primary cost drivers for the service (see CI Costing ). When associated with a price list item they define the default cost (see Price List).

View Cost Drivers
1. Log into the Service Desk console as Administrator, and open the Cost Driver workspace. The list of cost items comes up.
2. Double-click an item from the list to view details.
Create a Cost Driver
1. From the ITFM Cost Driver workspace toolbar, click New ITFM Cost Driver. A blank cost driver form appears.
2. Enter information into the fields.
-fields-
Field Description
Status Choose from the drop-down list: Active or Inactive.
Category Choose from the drop-down list: Allocation, Hardware, Software, Professional Service, Overhead, or Vendor Service.
Name Type a unique name for your cost driver item.
3. Click Save from the toolbar.

Link a Cost Driver to a Service

You can link a cost driver directly to a service, or link a service to a cost driver.

Linking a cost driver to a service

1. Log into the Service Desk console as Administrator, and open the ITFM Cost Driver workspace. A list of cost drivers defined for your organization appears.
2. Double-click an item to open the record. For example, Financial Services.
3. From the Service tab, click Link. A list of services appears.
4. Choose the service you want from the list, then click Select.
5. Click Save from the toolbar. The service appears on the Service list, and the cost driver appears within the service record under the Cost Driver tab.

Linking a service to a cost driver

1. Log into the Service Desk console as Administrator, and open the CI Service workspace. A list of services appears.
2. Double-click an item to open the record. For example, Email Service.
3. From the Cost Driver tab, , click Link. A list of cost drivers appears.
4. Choose the cost driver you want from the list, then click Select.
5. Click Save from the toolbar. The cost driver appears on the list, and the service item appears within the cost item record under the Service tab.
