Incident Escalations

Events are monitored by an escalation clock.

If an event does not occur within a prescribed period of time, an escalation action is executed. The exact timing and actions of an escalation depend on how your system is configured.

Escalations typically provide notification in a dashboard or an incident form. It is up to the Service Desk Analyst or Manager to take appropriate action based on such notifications.

Service Level Agreements (SLAs) associated with the affected incident Service override any system escalations. In the absence of Service Level Agreements, defined escalations are set to run.

If another Service is selected, the previous escalation clock is stopped and the records of the previous escalation schedule are marked Obsolete. A new escalation schedule goes into effect.

View Incident Escalations

You can view response and resolution escalations under the Resolution Details tab of the Incidents form.

Response and Resolution Targets

For details on each escalation, choose the Escalation Watch tab of the Incident form.

The Response Target uses the incident Response escalation schedule, and the Resolution Target uses the incident Resolution escalation schedule. If SLAs are configured for a Service, then the escalations that are configured within the SLA override the default system escalations. If another Service is selected, the previous escalation clock is stopped and the records of the previous escalation schedule is marked Obsolete. A new escalation schedule goes into effect.

If the Resolution Target bar or the escalation records on the Escalation Watch tab does not appear, click the Refresh button on the incident toolbar.
1. Within an open Incident, click the Escalation Watch tab. The list of escalations for the incident appear.
  • If the incident is logged from Self Service, but the response time has not been reached yet, the bar remains gray (since it is not determined whether the Response Target is Met or Breached).
  • If the incident changes from Logged to any other status (for example, Active), then the Response Target has been met, and the response bar indicates Met accordingly.
  • If the incident remains in Logged status and passes the response time, then the response bar indicates Breached.
  • After an incident Response escalation has been breached, even if the Status is updated to Active, the bar still indicates Breached, because the incident was not responded to in a timely manner.
  • The Resolution Target progress bar shows the resolution date and time for the incident, including the dates and time the incident is considered breached.
2. Select the Escalation Watch tab. Any configured escalations appear.

Escalations in the absence of SLAs

Double-click an item to view its details.

Incident Response

When an end user submits an incident through the Self Service module, its initial status is set to Logged. The incident status is changed to another state when an SDA assigns an owner. The amount of time that the incident is in the Logged state is the incident response time. If incident response time exceeds a prescribed duration, a notification appears on the Service Desk Management dashboard.

When you are notified of an incident response escalation:

1. Open the incident that was identified in the escalation notification. The incident Details appear.
2. In the Owner field, choose a name from the drop-down list.
3. Click Save from the toolbar.
  • The incident status changes to Active.
  • The incident is considered responded to and the escalation ends.
  • The end user is notified (if automatic notification is enabled).
