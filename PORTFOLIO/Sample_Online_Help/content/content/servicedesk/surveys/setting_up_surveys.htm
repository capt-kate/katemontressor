Setting Up Surveys

Customer surveys measure customer satisfaction with the various departments in your organization. Surveys are published over the web, and distributed by email. When opened, they appear in a new browser window.

By default, a Service Desk Manager can view survey results within the business layouts.

Surveys are triggered upon the conclusion of a specific event, such as after closing an incident. This automated process is defined in a business rule associated with a specific business object.

Three types of surveys can be created:

  • Post-Transaction. This survey type is event-driven, enabling you to send a survey triggered by the conclusion of a specified event, such as after every 5th call received. The sending of post-transaction surveys is automated.
  • On-Demand. This survey type is used to survey the entire customer base (or some specified segment of customers) on an as-needed basis.
  • Periodic. The same parameters as the On-Demand survey, however, unlike the On-Demand survey, which can be submitted only once, the Periodic survey can be completed and submitted multiple times by the respondent.

 

Create a Survey
Incident and service request survey notifications are generated for every fifth record. This is implemented with the workflow and is controlled by a global constant.
1. From the Configuration Console Settings pane under Configure > Communication Templates, click Surveys. The Survey List appears.
2. Click Create a new survey. The Create Survey form appears.
3. Enter information into the fields.
-fields-
Field Description

Survey title

Enter a unique title for the survey. This name is saved as a global constant for use when creating a link in a survey notification email. This name will be displayed as a header on each page of the survey.

Survey type

on-demand (only survey type available in HEAT).

Logo (URL)

Use to show your company logo on the survey by inserting the logo local web server path or public URL.

For example,

a local durvey web server path would be: ../templates/images/logo.png

a public URL would be: http://www.mycompany.com/logo-small.gif

Style

Select the color style to apply to the survey.

Description

Optional. Enter a description about the survey which appears as a tool tip for the survey title on the Survey List page.

Welcome message

Optional. Enter a message to greet the survey taker. Appears on the Welcome page of the survey.

Thank you message

Enter a message to display when the survey is submitted. If the respondent tries to resubmit a survey, the thank you message appears as a reminder that the survey has been previously submitted.

Suspend message

Enter the message that appears if the survey is suspended (unpublished).

Expired on

Enter an expiration date or click the calendar to select an expiration date; if the respondent tries to submit the survey on or after the expiration date, the expiration message appears.

Expiration message

Enter a message that appears if the respondent attempts to submit a survey on or after the expiration date.
4. Click Save. The blank survey appears.

The following command items are available on the survey:

survey list

Return to the Survey List page.

copy survey

Duplicate the current survey.

survey properties

The basic survey properties described above.

delete survey

Click to delete the current survey. This action can not be undone.

add question

Click to add a question to the survey.

preview

Click to see what the user will encounter.
Add Survey Questions
1. Add questions to the survey. Click Add question. The Add Question form appears.
2. Enter information into the fields.
-fields-
Fields Description

Question Type

Select a format from the drop-down list.

  • text - The respondent can enter a short, one-line answer.
  • essay - The respondent can enter a long answer; this is essentially a comments field.
  • radio button - The respondent can only select one answer.
  • check box - The respondent can select multiple answers.

Question

Enter a survey question.

Answer Required

Select to require the user to respond to the question.

Question Category

Choose from the drop-down list.

Answers

This field is used for radio button or check box questions.

Type the response choices in the Answers field. Each line in the answer box represents an answer option, so press Enter to start a new line for each response.

For example, for a radio button or checkbox, you can enter:

  • Excellent [weight=5]
  • Very Good [weight=4]
  • Good [weight=3]
  • Fair [weight=2]
  • Poor [weight=1]
  • Don't Know/Not Applicable [weight=5]

Or for a Yes/No or True/False response for a radio button or checkbox, you can enter:

  • Yes [weight=1]
  • No [weight=0]

Last answer contains text box

Select to provide a short answer field as a final response option (for example, an Other option or a Comments box).
3. Click Save.
4. Repeat these steps to add more questions to the survey.

The questions appear as you add each one.

  • Click insert page break to create a page break between questions.
  • Click insert question to place a question between two existing questions.
5. After all questions have been entered and you are ready to publish the survey, go to the survey list and click Start.
To suspend a survey, click Stop.

 

Question Categories

On the Survey List page, click the Question Categories list to add general groupings for use in sorting survey data.

Enter question categories in the fields provided, such as Courtesy or Timeliness of Resolution. These categories are available when adding questions to a survey. If a category is used, it appears in the Used list on the Question Categories page.

You cannot delete a question category if it is used on an active survey.

To add a category, click Add.

 

Duplicate a Survey
1. On the Survey List page, click the name of the survey to copy. The survey opens.
2. To copy the survey, click the copy survey link at the top of the page. The Source survey field confirms the survey being copied.
3. Enter your information into the fields.
New survey title Enter a name for the new survey.
New survey description Optional. Enter a description for the new survey. The description will appear as a tool tip when you move your mouse over a survey title on the Survey List page.
4. Click Copy. The new survey opens.
5. Make any needed changes to the questions and survey properties, then Save.

 

Modify a Survey

You cannot edit a survey once responses have been received for that survey. If you clear the responses, you can then edit the survey.

Within the survey page, you can perform the following actions:

Action Description
Edit Click to edit a question on the page; to the right of the question.
Sort Click to change the order of the questions; to the right of the question.

Move Up

Move Down

 Reposition the order of the questions.
Remove Click to delete a question from the survey, then click OK to confirm.
Remove Page Break Click the link to the right of the page break to remove.
Add Question

To add a question to the end of the survey, click the link at the top of the page;

To add a question to a specific location in the survey, click Insert Question at the bottom of each question segment.
Survey Properties Click to edit the survey properties (survey type, welcome message, etc.).
Copy Survey Click to copy the open survey.
Delete Survey Click to delete the open survey.
Preview Click to see a preview of the open survey.
Survey List Click to return to the Survey List page.
If the survey has responses, you will not see the delete survey link. You must first delete the responses from the database by clicking the clear link on the Survey List page.

 

Delete a Survey

You cannot delete a survey that has responses. When you delete a survey from the database, all data related to that survey is also deleted.

1. On the Survey List page, click the name of the survey to delete. The survey opens.
2. To delete the survey, click the delete survey link at the top of the page.
3. Click OK to confirm the deletion.
