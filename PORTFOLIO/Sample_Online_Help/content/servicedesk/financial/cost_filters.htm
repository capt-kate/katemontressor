Cost Filters

Filter by time

You can view costs that occur within a specific time period using Cost quick filters. For example, you could view only the costs that have occurred in the past three months.

1. Log into the Service Desk console as Administrator, open the CI Service workspace, then open the service record whose costs you want to see. For example, CRM Service.
2. From the Cost Item tab, choose the defined time period from the drop-down list, or enter the start and end dates into the date fields. When you choose a defined time period, the start and end dates automatically appear in the fields. Click Filter.

Only the costs incurred during the specified time appear in the cost pane.

3. To remove the filter, click Clear Filter.
Filter by type

You can view only those costs you want to see filtered by certain types. For example, you can view only fixed costs.

1. Log into the Service Desk console as Administrator, open the CI Service workspace, then open the service record whose costs you want to see. For example, CRM Service.
2. From the Cost Item tab, filter one of the columns. For example, under the Capital Expense column, filter by Yes to view only Capital Expense items. (See Filtering Data and Recordsd.)

The items in the cost pane are listed according to the filtered column.

Group by Cost category

You can view cost sub-totals based on how the data is grouped.

1. Log into the Service Desk console as Administrator, open the CI Service workspace, then open the service record whose costs you want to see. For example, CRM Service.
2. From the Cost Item tab, group one of the columns. For example, Capital Expense or Fixed (costs). (See Grouping Records.)

The items in the cost pane are listed according to the grouped column.

Separate by primary cost drivers

You can view costs according to primary cost drivers.

1. Log into the Service Desk console as Administrator, open the CI Service workspace, then open the service record whose costs you want to see. For example, CRM Service.
2. From the Cost Item tab, click Separate total for primary cost drivers from the right pane.

Primary Cost Totals are Separated and Sub-totaled

The primary costs are separated and sub-totaled, and other costs are also listed.

3. To change a cost from Other Cost to Primary Cost, click Link .

Link to Other Costs

The cost item moves from the Other Costs area up to the Cost Driver area and onto the list in the Cost Driver tab.
